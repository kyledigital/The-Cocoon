A small story with a big heart

Kip the caterpillar begins to feel different. He enters the cocoon unsure of what is happening, and in the quiet of that strange new space he learns something gentle and true: change does not always feel loud or clear. Sometimes it feels still, confusing, and beautiful at the same time.

Made for tender moments

The Cocoon is designed to be simple enough for a child to understand and meaningful enough for a parent, teacher, or grown reader to hold onto long after the page is turned.

Core themes

The heart of the story rests on four gentle ideas children can carry with them.

1

Change

Growing can feel unfamiliar, but that does not mean something is wrong.

2

Feelings

Quiet feelings, confused feelings, and tender feelings all deserve room.

3

Growth

Transformation often happens slowly, softly, and from the inside out.

4

Hope

Even when the path feels unclear, there is beauty waiting on the other side.
